1. Sign Convention (New Cartesian)

All distances measured from the pole (mirror) or optical centre (lens).
Positive = in direction of incident light (usually right). Negative = opposite to incident light (usually left).
QuantitySignReason
Object distance uAlways − (real object)Object is on incoming side (left)
Real image distance v− (mirror), + (lens)Real image: same side as object (mirror), opposite side (lens)
Virtual image distance v+ (mirror), − (lens)Virtual image: behind mirror, same side as object for lens
Focal length (concave mirror)− (negative)Focus is on same side as object (real focus)
Focal length (convex mirror)+ (positive)Virtual focus — behind mirror
Focal length (convex lens)+ (positive)Converging
Focal length (concave lens)− (negative)Diverging
The #1 trap in optics: Mirror formula and lens formula look similar but have DIFFERENT sign implications because real images form on different sides. Write the formula first, then substitute values with correct signs.

2. Plane Mirror

Key Properties
  • Image is virtual, erect, laterally inverted, same size (m = +1)
  • Image is as far behind mirror as object is in front
  • Minimum mirror length to see full image = half the height of person
  • If mirror rotates by θ, reflected ray rotates by 2θ
  • Two mirrors at angle α: number of images = 360°/α − 1 (when 360/α is integer)

3. Spherical Mirrors

Relation: f = R/2 — focal length is half the radius of curvature.
Concave mirror: converging (f < 0 in sign convention). Convex mirror: diverging (f > 0).

4. Mirror Formula & Magnification

Mirror formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f = 2/R
Magnification: m = −v/u = h_i/h_o
🔢 Interactive — Mirror / Lens Calculator

Principal ray diagram — updates with the calculator above. Dashed = virtual rays.

Interpreting Magnification
  • m < 0: image is inverted (real image for concave mirror)
  • m > 0: image is erect (virtual image)
  • |m| > 1: image is enlarged
  • |m| < 1: image is diminished
  • m = −1: equal size, inverted, real image (object at C for concave mirror)

5. Convex Mirror — Always Virtual

Convex mirror ALWAYS forms a virtual, erect, diminished image regardless of object position. Used as rear-view mirror (wide field of view).
Why Convex Mirrors are Used as Rear-view Mirrors

Always virtual + erect image → appears in front of driver. Always diminished → wider field of view than plane mirror. Image is always between focus and pole (compact image, entire scene fits).

6. Snell's Law of Refraction

n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
n = refractive index = c/v (c = speed of light in vacuum, v = speed in medium). At boundary, frequency stays constant; speed and wavelength change.
PropertyRelation
Speed in mediumv = c/n
Wavelength in mediumλ_medium = λ_vacuum/n
Frequencyf = constant (never changes)
Relative refractive indexn₁₂ = n₂/n₁ = sin θ₁/sin θ₂
Denser medium (higher n): slower speed, shorter wavelength, ray bends TOWARD normal. Rarer medium (lower n): faster speed, longer wavelength, ray bends AWAY from normal.

Refraction at interface: ray bends toward normal entering denser medium (n₂ > n₁). θ₂ < θ₁.

7. Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

Conditions: 1) Ray goes from denser to rarer medium (n₁ > n₂) 2) Angle of incidence ≥ critical angle θ_c
Critical angle: sin θ_c = n₂/n₁ = 1/n (when n₂ = 1 for air)
Applications of TIR
  • Optical fibres (communication, medicine)
  • Totally reflecting prisms (periscopes, binoculars)
  • Diamonds sparkle (high n → small θ_c → TIR inside)
  • Mirage formation
TIR Key Facts
  • θ_c smaller → TIR easier at smaller angles
  • Diamond: n=2.42, θ_c = 24.4° (very small → easily TIR)
  • Glass-air: n=1.5, θ_c = 41.8°
  • Water-air: n=1.33, θ_c = 48.7°

8. Apparent Depth & Shift

Apparent depth = Real depth / n (viewing from rarer into denser medium)
Normal shift (upward shift of image) = t(1 − 1/n) where t = thickness of slab.
Glass Slab — Lateral Shift

Lateral shift = d = t sinθ(1 − cosθ/√(n²−sin²θ))

For near-normal incidence (small θ): d ≈ t sinθ(1 − 1/n)

The emergent ray is parallel to the incident ray (same direction, shifted sideways).

Lens formula: 1/v − 1/u = 1/f

Note the MINUS sign: 1/v − 1/u (different from mirror formula 1/v + 1/u)

10. Lens Maker's Equation

1/f = (n − 1)(1/R₁ − 1/R₂)
n = refractive index of lens material · R₁ = radius of first surface · R₂ = radius of second surface (signs according to convention)
Sign Convention for Radii

R₁: centre of curvature of first surface (the one facing the incoming light)

R₂: centre of curvature of second surface

If centre of curvature is to the right (same side as outgoing light): R is positive. If to the left: R is negative.

Example: Convex lens (both surfaces bulging outward): R₁ > 0, R₂ < 0 → 1/R₁ − 1/R₂ > 0 → f > 0 ✓

11. Lens Power and Combination

P = 1/f (f in metres, P in dioptres D)
Lenses in contact: P = P₁ + P₂ or 1/f = 1/f₁ + 1/f₂

12. Prisms & Dispersion

n = sin((A + δ_m)/2) / sin(A/2)
A = prism angle, δ_m = angle of minimum deviation. At minimum deviation: i₁ = i₂, r₁ = r₂ = A/2.
FormulaMeaning
δ = (i₁ + i₂) − ADeviation through prism
r₁ + r₂ = ASum of refraction angles = prism angle
n = sin((A+δ_m)/2)/sin(A/2)At minimum deviation
δ_m = 2i₁ − AMinimum deviation condition
Dispersion of White Light

Different wavelengths have different refractive indices. n_violet > n_red (violet bends more).

VIBGYOR — violet deviates most, red least. Angle of dispersion = δ_V − δ_R.

Dispersive power: ω = (n_V − n_R)/(n_Y − 1) where n_Y is mean refractive index.

Fringe width: β = λD/d
λ = wavelength · D = distance to screen · d = slit separation

If medium is changed (e.g., filled with water, n=1.33): λ_medium = λ_air/n → fringe width β = λ_air×D/(n×d). Fringe width DECREASES in denser medium.

14. Single Slit Diffraction

First minimum: a sinθ = λ → sinθ ≈ θ = λ/a
a = slit width. Width of central maximum = 2λD/a.
Key difference between YDSE and diffraction: YDSE uses TWO slits (d = separation between slits). Single slit diffraction uses ONE slit (a = width of slit). Do not confuse d and a.

15. Worked Examples

Example 1 — Concave Mirror

Object 15 cm from concave mirror of focal length 10 cm. Find image position and magnification.

Given: f = −10 cm, u = −15 cm (real object)
Mirror formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f
1/v + 1/(−15) = 1/(−10)
1/v = −1/10 + 1/15 = −3/30 + 2/30 = −1/30
v = −30 cm (real, same side as object → in front of mirror ✓)
m = −v/u = −(−30)/(−15) = −2 (inverted, magnified 2×)
Example 2 — Convex Lens

Object 30 cm from convex lens of focal length 20 cm. Find image.

Given: f = +20 cm, u = −30 cm
Lens formula: 1/v − 1/u = 1/f
1/v − 1/(−30) = 1/20
1/v + 1/30 = 1/20
1/v = 1/20 − 1/30 = 3/60 − 2/60 = 1/60
v = +60 cm (real, on other side of lens ✓)
m = v/u = 60/(−30) = −2 (inverted, 2× magnified)
Example 3 — YDSE

YDSE: λ=500 nm, d=0.5 mm, D=2 m. Find fringe width and position of 5th bright fringe.

β = λD/d = (500×10⁻⁹ × 2) / (0.5×10⁻³) = 1000×10⁻⁹/0.5×10⁻³ = 2×10⁻³ m = 2 mm
5th bright fringe: y₅ = 5β = 5 × 2 = 10 mm
Example 4 — Critical Angle

Find critical angle for glass-air interface. n_glass = 1.5. What happens at 45°?

sin θ_c = 1/n = 1/1.5 = 0.667
θ_c = arcsin(0.667) = 41.8°
At 45° > 41.8° → TIR occurs. No refraction, complete reflection.

16. Common JEE Traps

Trap 1: Mirror formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f. Lens formula: 1/v − 1/u = 1/f. The MINUS is only in the lens formula. Getting this wrong causes systematic errors.
Trap 2: Mirror magnification: m = −v/u. Lens magnification: m = +v/u. Sign flip between mirror and lens.
Trap 3: Object distance u is ALWAYS negative for real objects (in New Cartesian). Students often write u as positive — this always gives wrong answers.
Trap 4: YDSE fringe width β = λD/d: d is the SEPARATION between slits, D is the DISTANCE to screen. Do not confuse these. If D triples, β triples.
Trap 5: In YDSE with dark fringes: path difference = (2n−1)λ/2 where n = 1, 2, 3… (not 0). First dark fringe (n=1): Δ = λ/2.
Trap 6: Frequency never changes on refraction. Only speed (v = c/n) and wavelength (λ = λ₀/n) change.
Trap 7: TIR: ray must go from DENSER to RARER medium (not the other way). A ray going from air into glass can never undergo TIR.
Trap 8: Power of lens combination in contact: P = P₁ + P₂. But if there's a mirror: P_eq = 2P_L + P_M (light passes through lens TWICE, mirror once).
Trap 9: Minimum deviation through prism: rays inside are parallel to base (for equilateral prism). The formula n = sin((A+δ_m)/2)/sin(A/2) is ONLY valid at minimum deviation.
