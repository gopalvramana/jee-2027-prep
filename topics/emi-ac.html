1. Magnetic Flux

Φ = B · A = BA cosθ
θ = angle between B and the NORMAL to the surface (NOT the angle between B and the surface plane). Unit: Weber (Wb = T·m²).
Ways to Change Flux → Induce EMF
  • Change B: move a magnet in/out of a coil
  • Change A: expand or contract the area of a conducting loop
  • Change θ: rotate the coil in the field (generator principle)
  • All three produce the same effect — EMF = −dΦ/dt

2. Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction

ε = −N dΦ/dt
Magnitude of induced EMF = N × rate of change of flux through a single turn. The negative sign encodes Lenz's law.
FormFormulaUse When
Generalε = −N dΦ/dtFlux changing with time
Average (finite ΔΦ, Δt)ε = N ΔΦ/ΔtFor total flux change
Induced currentI = ε/R = N(dΦ/dt)/RCircuit resistance known
Charge flowedq = NΔΦ/RIndependent of time taken!
Charge = NΔΦ/R — independent of how fast the flux changes. Only the total flux change matters for total charge. Resistance determines charge for a given flux change.

3. Lenz's Law

The induced current opposes the change in flux that caused it.
This is a consequence of energy conservation — a "free" current would violate conservation of energy.
Applying Lenz's Law Step by Step
  1. Identify the direction of the existing magnetic flux through the loop
  2. Determine if flux is increasing or decreasing
  3. Induced current must create B field that OPPOSES this change:
    • Flux increasing into page → induced B must point OUT of page → induced current counterclockwise (viewed from front)
    • Flux decreasing into page → induced B must point INTO page → induced current clockwise
  4. Apply right-hand rule to find current direction from induced B direction

4. Motional EMF

ε = BLv
A conductor of length L moving at velocity v perpendicular to field B. The free electrons experience Lorentz force → charge separation → EMF.
Motional EMF — Detailed Analysis
  • Works only when v ⊥ B ⊥ L (all three mutually perpendicular)
  • If circuit is closed with resistance R: I = BLv/R
  • Power dissipated: P = B²L²v²/R
  • Force required to maintain constant v: F = BIL = B²L²v/R (opposing — Lenz's law)
  • If v varies: ε = BL(dv/dt × t) → use ε = Bv(t)·L at each instant
Rotating rod EMF: A rod of length L rotating about one end at ω in field B (perpendicular to rod): ε = ½BωL². Different from BLv!

5. Eddy Currents

Induced currents in bulk conductors (not just wires) — circulate in closed loops within the material itself.
Harmful Effects
  • Energy loss as heat in transformer cores
  • Minimised by using laminated cores
  • Power loss in AC machines
Useful Applications
  • Electromagnetic braking (trains, roller coasters)
  • Induction cooktops
  • Metal detectors
  • Magnetic damping in galvanometers

6. Self-Inductance

L is defined by: ε = −L dI/dt
L = self-inductance (Henry, H). When current through a coil changes, the changing flux through it induces an EMF that opposes the change.
FormulaMeaning
ε = −L dI/dtEMF induced by changing I
L = μ₀n²V = μ₀n²AlSelf-inductance of solenoid (V = volume)
U = ½LI²Energy stored in inductor
L_series = L₁ + L₂Series (no mutual inductance)
1/L_parallel = 1/L₁ + 1/L₂Parallel (no mutual inductance)
Inductor opposes CHANGES in current. At t=0, inductor acts like open circuit (prevents sudden current change). After long time, acts like wire (short circuit) — full DC current flows.

7. Mutual Inductance

ε₂ = −M dI₁/dt
M = mutual inductance between two coils. When current in coil 1 changes, it induces EMF in coil 2.
FormulaNotes
M = k√(L₁L₂)k = coupling coefficient (0 ≤ k ≤ 1)
M = μ₀N₁N₂A/lFor coaxial solenoids of same area

8. Transformer

V₁/V₂ = N₁/N₂ (voltage ratio = turns ratio)
V₁I₁ = V₂I₂ (power conservation in ideal transformer)
TypeTurnsEffect
Step-upN₂ > N₁V₂ > V₁, I₂ < I₁ (voltage up, current down)
Step-downN₂ < N₁V₂ < V₁, I₂ > I₁ (voltage down, current up)
Efficiency losses in real transformers: eddy currents (minimised by laminated core), hysteresis loss (use soft iron core), copper loss (I²R in windings), flux leakage.

11. R, L, C Separately in AC

ELI the ICE man: In an inductive circuit (L), E(voltage) leads I(current) — ELI. In a capacitive circuit (C), I leads E — ICE. The extra letter is the leading quantity.

12. LCR Series Circuit

Z = √[R² + (X_L − X_C)²]
tan φ = (X_L − X_C) / R (φ = phase angle of V relative to I)

13. Resonance in LCR Circuit

At resonance: X_L = X_C → ω₀ = 1/√(LC)
At ω₀: Z = R (minimum), I = V/R (maximum), power factor = 1, V and I are in phase.
Quantity at ResonanceValue
Impedance ZZ = R (minimum)
Current II = V/R (maximum)
Phase angle φφ = 0° (in phase)
Power factorcos φ = 1 (maximum)
V_L = V_C (individually)V_L = V_C = I × X_L (can be ≫ supply voltage!)
Q-factor (sharpness)Q = ω₀L/R = 1/(ω₀CR) = (1/R)√(L/C)
Resonant frequency f₀f₀ = 1/(2π√LC) Hz
At resonance V_L = V_C but they are 180° out of phase — they cancel! The net voltage across L+C = 0. All supply voltage appears across R. But individually, V_L and V_C can be much larger than supply voltage (voltage magnification = Q-factor).

P = V_rms × I_rms × cos φ = I²_rms × R
cos φ = power factor = R/Z. Only the resistive component dissipates real power.

15. Worked Examples

Example 1 — Faraday's Law (Average EMF)

A coil of 200 turns and area 0.05 m² is placed in a magnetic field that changes uniformly from 0 to 0.8 T in 0.4 s. Find induced EMF and current if resistance = 20 Ω.

ΔΦ = BA = 0.8 × 0.05 = 0.04 Wb (per turn)
ε = N × ΔΦ/Δt = 200 × 0.04/0.4 = 20 V
I = ε/R = 20/20 = 1 A
Example 2 — Motional EMF

A rod of length 0.5 m moves at 4 m/s perpendicular to B = 0.3 T. The circuit has resistance 6 Ω. Find EMF, current, and the force needed to maintain constant velocity.

ε = BLv = 0.3 × 0.5 × 4 = 0.6 V
I = ε/R = 0.6/6 = 0.1 A
Force opposing motion = BIL = 0.3 × 0.1 × 0.5 = 0.015 N
Example 3 — LCR at Resonance

Series LCR: R=10Ω, L=100mH, C=100μF, V=100V. Find f₀, I at resonance, and Q-factor.

ω₀ = 1/√(LC) = 1/√(0.1 × 100×10⁻⁶) = 1/√(10⁻⁵) = 1/3.162×10⁻³ ≈ 316 rad/s
f₀ = ω₀/2π = 316/6.28 ≈ 50.3 Hz
I = V/R = 100/10 = 10 A
Q = ω₀L/R = 316×0.1/10 = 3.16

16. Common JEE Traps

Trap 1: Total charge = NΔΦ/R — independent of TIME taken. The rate (EMF) depends on time, but total charge depends only on total flux change.
Trap 2: θ in Φ = BA cosθ — θ is angle between B and NORMAL to area, NOT between B and the surface plane.
Trap 3: V_L and V_C at resonance are NOT zero — they can be much larger than supply voltage V. Only V_L − V_C = 0 (they cancel). V_L = V_C = QV (Q-factor × supply voltage).
Trap 4: Pure inductor or capacitor: average power = 0, NOT V_rms × I_rms. P_avg = V_rms × I_rms × cos φ and cos 90° = 0.
Trap 5: Step-up transformer increases voltage but DECREASES current (energy conservation). If V doubles, I halves. Never both increase simultaneously.
Trap 6: At t=0, an inductor acts as OPEN circuit (opposes sudden current). After very long time, inductor acts as SHORT circuit (zero reactance for DC). Opposite of capacitor.
Trap 7: Motional EMF for rotating rod = ½BωL² (NOT BωL²). Factor of ½ because the effective average distance from pivot is L/2.
Trap 8: X_L = ωL increases with ω; X_C = 1/ωC decreases with ω. At high frequency, capacitor is low impedance (passes current), inductor is high impedance (blocks). Opposite at low frequency.
