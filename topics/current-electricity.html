Conventional current direction = opposite to electron flow. Electrons move from − to + (inside battery), but conventional current flows from + to −. JEE uses conventional current direction.

2. Ohm's Law & V-I Characteristics

V = IR — valid for ohmic conductors at constant temperature. V-I graph is a straight line through origin with slope = R.
Ohmic Conductors
  • R is constant (at constant T)
  • V-I graph: straight line
  • Metals at moderate temperatures
  • R = V/I = constant
Non-Ohmic Conductors
  • R varies with V or I
  • V-I graph: curved
  • Diodes, filament bulbs, semiconductors
  • R = V/I (calculated at each point)

3. Resistivity and R = ρL/A

R = ρL/A
ρ = resistivity (material property, Ω·m) · L = length (m) · A = cross-sectional area (m²)
Key Relationships — Stretching/Drawing Wire
  • Volume is conserved: A₁L₁ = A₂L₂ = constant
  • If length doubles (L₂ = 2L₁): A₂ = A₁/2, so R₂ = ρ×2L₁/(A₁/2) = 4R₁ (quadruples!)
  • If radius doubles: A quadruples → R becomes ¼
  • General: if length becomes n times → R becomes n² times
JEE Trap: When a wire is stretched to n times its original length, its resistance increases by factor n² (not n), because BOTH L increases AND A decreases.

4. Temperature Dependence of Resistance

R = R₀(1 + αΔT)
α = temperature coefficient of resistance · ΔT = temperature change from reference T₀
Conductors (Metals)
  • α > 0 (positive)
  • R increases with temperature
  • More collisions at higher T
  • Example: Copper, Aluminium
Semiconductors
  • α < 0 (negative)
  • R decreases with temperature
  • More charge carriers at higher T
  • Example: Silicon, Germanium

5. Series Combination

R_series = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + …
Same current flows through all; voltage divides proportionally to resistance.
PropertySeries
CurrentI same everywhere
VoltageV = V₁ + V₂ + V₃ = I(R₁+R₂+R₃)
PowerP = I²R — higher R dissipates MORE power
Equivalent RR_eq = R₁ + R₂ + … (always larger than any individual R)
Voltage Divider: V₁ = V × R₁/(R₁+R₂) — voltage across R₁ is proportional to R₁.

6. Parallel Combination

1/R_parallel = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + …
Same voltage across all; current divides inversely proportional to resistance.
PropertyParallel
VoltageV same across all branches
CurrentI = I₁ + I₂ + I₃; I₁/I₂ = R₂/R₁
PowerP = V²/R — lower R dissipates MORE power
Equivalent RR_eq always LESS than smallest individual R
Two Resistors in Parallel — Quick Formula

R_eq = R₁R₂/(R₁+R₂) — product over sum. Only works for exactly two resistors!

Current divider: I₁ = I × R₂/(R₁+R₂) — current in R₁ is proportional to R₂ (the OTHER one).

7. Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)

ΣI_in = ΣI_out at any junction
Based on conservation of charge. Charge cannot accumulate at a node.
How to Apply KCL
  1. Label each branch with a current variable (I₁, I₂, …) with an assumed direction
  2. At each junction: sum of incoming currents = sum of outgoing
  3. A negative answer means the actual direction is opposite to assumed
  4. For N junctions, you get N−1 independent KCL equations

8. Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)

ΣV = 0 around any closed loop
Based on conservation of energy. Going around a loop and returning, net potential change = 0.
KVL Sign Convention (Clockwise traversal)
  • Cross a resistor in the direction of assumed current → voltage drop: −IR
  • Cross a resistor against assumed current → voltage gain: +IR
  • Cross a battery from − to + (low to high potential) → +ε
  • Cross a battery from + to − (high to low potential) → −ε
Systematic approach: Assume currents in all branches. Write KCL at N−1 junctions + KVL for sufficient loops. Solve simultaneously. Never guess and check.

9. Wheatstone Bridge

Balanced condition: P/Q = R/S
When balanced: no current through the galvanometer (middle branch). The bridge resistance = (P+Q)(R+S)/(P+Q+R+S) but with P+Q parallel to R+S.
Wheatstone Bridge — Solving Approach

If balanced (P/Q = R/S): ignore galvanometer branch. Solve as two series strings in parallel.

If unbalanced: must use Kirchhoff's laws with 3 unknowns and 2 loops.

JEE almost always gives balanced bridge problems — check the ratio first!

Meter Bridge — Based on Wheatstone Principle

X = R × l / (100 − l)

X = unknown resistance, R = known resistance, l = balance length from left end (cm)

At balance: X/R = l/(100−l) → same as Wheatstone balance condition.

11. Cells in Series and Parallel

Series Cells
  • ε_total = ε₁ + ε₂ + … (EMFs add)
  • r_total = r₁ + r₂ + … (resistances add)
  • Same current through all cells
  • Best for: high voltage applications
Parallel Cells (n identical)
  • ε_total = ε (same as single cell)
  • r_total = r/n (reduced internal R)
  • Best for: high current applications
  • Each cell supplies I/n current
Cells opposing each other in series: ε_net = ε₁ − ε₂ if connected +to+, r_net = r₁ + r₂. Higher EMF cell drives current through lower EMF cell (charging it).

12. Metre Bridge

X = R × l / (100 − l)
l = balance length (cm), R = known resistance in opposite gap, X = unknown resistance.
Metre Bridge — End Error & Sensitivity
  • Balance point near 50 cm = most sensitive (equal arms)
  • Avoid balance point below 20 cm or above 80 cm (large % error)
  • End error: zero of scale may not coincide with end of wire — must use end corrections
  • Shift R to bring balance point near midpoint

13. Potentiometer

Principle: No current is drawn from the cell being measured at balance (null method). This makes it more accurate than a voltmeter.
ApplicationFormula
Compare EMFsE₁/E₂ = l₁/l₂
Measure internal resistancer = R(l₁ − l₂)/l₂
Potential gradientk = V/L (V/m along wire)
Internal Resistance Measurement

l₁ = balance length with cell on open circuit (no external R)

l₂ = balance length with external resistance R connected across cell

Then: r = R(l₁ − l₂)/l₂

Derived from: E/V = l₁/l₂, and V = E − Ir = ER/(R+r) → solve for r.

14. Electrical Power

FormulaBest Used WhenNote
P = VIBoth V and I knownMost general
P = I²RCurrent and resistance knownUse for series: same I
P = V²/RVoltage and resistance knownUse for parallel: same V
Bulb brightness trick:
Series: same I → P = I²R → higher R = brighter (more voltage across it)
Parallel: same V → P = V²/R → lower R = brighter (more current through it)
Rated Power vs Actual Power

A bulb rated "60 W at 220 V" has R = V²/P = 220²/60 ≈ 807 Ω.

If connected to 110 V: P_actual = V²/R = 110²/807 ≈ 15 W (much dimmer).

JEE frequently asks: which bulb is brighter when connected in series/parallel.

15. Worked Examples

Example 1 — Equivalent Resistance

Find equivalent resistance between A and B: 2Ω and 3Ω in series, this combination in parallel with 6Ω. Connected to 12 V battery.

Series combination: 2 + 3 = 5 Ω
Parallel: R_eq = 5×6/(5+6) = 30/11 ≈ 2.73 Ω
Total current I = 12/2.73 ≈ 4.4 A
Current in 6Ω branch: I₆ = 12/6 = 2 A
Current in 5Ω branch: I₅ = 12/5 = 2.4 A
Example 2 — Wheatstone Bridge

Wheatstone bridge: P=3Ω, Q=6Ω, R=4Ω. Find S for balance. What is the resistance of the bridge between the supply terminals?

Balance condition: P/Q = R/S → 3/6 = 4/S → S = 8 Ω
At balance: bridge = (P+Q) ∥ (R+S) = 9 ∥ 12 = 9×12/(9+12) = 108/21 ≈ 5.14 Ω
Example 3 — Potentiometer

Potentiometer wire 10 m. A cell of EMF 1.5 V balances at 3 m. What is the EMF that balances at 2.4 m?

Potential gradient k = 1.5/3 = 0.5 V/m
E₂ = k × l₂ = 0.5 × 2.4 = 1.2 V

16. Common JEE Traps

Trap 1: R_parallel is LESS than the smallest individual resistor. If you get a larger value, you added instead of applied 1/R formula.
Trap 2: For bulbs in SERIES — higher rated wattage bulb has LOWER resistance (rated at higher V). At same V rating: P = V²/R, so higher P means lower R.
Trap 3: Terminal voltage during charging is V = E + Ir (GREATER than EMF), not V = E − Ir.
Trap 4: Stretching a wire of length L to 2L: resistance becomes 4R, not 2R. Both L doubles AND A halves.
Trap 5: Metre bridge balance formula: X = R×l/(100−l). It's (100−l) NOT l in denominator for the right-side gap.
Trap 6: KVL sign — when traversing a loop in the direction of assumed current through a resistor, it's a DROP (−IR), not a rise.
Trap 7: Potentiometer measures EMF (open circuit) not terminal voltage. The null method ensures no current flows through the cell being measured.
Trap 8: Wheatstone bridge balance P/Q = R/S means the ratio in the SAME arm (top-left : top-right = bottom-left : bottom-right). Mixing up arms gives wrong answer.
